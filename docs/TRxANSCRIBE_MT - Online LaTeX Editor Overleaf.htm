The common construction for encoder and decoder in Seq2Seq module includes two main types: recurrent
neural networks (RNN) (with Long Short Term Memory (LSTM) \cite{hochreiter1997long} or with Gated
Recurrent Unit (GRU) \cite{chung2014empirical}) and convolutional neural networks (CNN)
\cite{kim2014convolutional}.
Prior work has many exploration on Seq2Seq structure. Two RNN units are firstly used to encode source
sentences into fixed-length hidden representations which then decoded into target sentences
\cite{DBLP:journals/corr/ChoMGBSB14}. Deep LSTM-based RNN units employed in both encoder and decoder was
shown to increase the capacity of model\cite{DBLP:journals/corr/WuSCLNMKCGMKSJL16}.
\cite{DBLP:journals/corr/GehringAGYD17} introduced CNN units and attention mechanism to use shorter path
learning long dependence compared with recurrent chain structure in RNN. Nevertheless, their memory is
typically small and might not be enough for the transcription, where one is confronted with long and
complicated sentences.
In recent work, \cite{DBLP:journals/corr/VaswaniSPUJGKP17} develops novel Transformer architecture
purely making use of attention mechanism without recurrence or convolution entirely, which shown
significant improvement in most of natural language processing related tasks.
To our knowledge, this work is first to do direct and automatic transcription from prescription
directions to pharmacy directions using transformer based neural machine translation in medical domain.
\section{Neural Text Transcription(NTT)}
\begin{figure}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{image/Architecture.png}
\caption{Pipeline of Neural Text Transcription(left) and Global Attention mechanism (right)}
\label{pipline}
\end{figure}
Our model, Neural Text Transcription (NTT) automatically transcribe e-prescription into completed,
normalized, simplified pharmacy directions. Figure \ref{pipline}(left) shows pipeline of our NTT model
with four main components. For main neural machine translation, similar to the other Seq2Seq
architecture, we implemented encoder and decoder structure using Transformer module (Section
\ref{Transformer}). Encoder maps source sequential context input $[x_1, x_2,...,x_n]$ into hidden state
vector representation $[h_1,h_2,...h_n]$. Given hidden states, decoder generated target output
$[y_1,y_2,...y_m]$ word by word with consideration of the global attention weighted input information.
majority voting of output from several independent trained machine translation model is applied to
reduce possibility of random error from parameterized model (Section \ref{majority}). Numerical Checker
makes the choice between source e-prescription and target pharmacy directions after detecting whether
there is any making up numerical information error during transcription (Section \ref{Checker}).
Rule-based normalization section follows to complete, normalize and simplify final e-prescriptions under
expertise of pharmacy specialists (Section \ref{Regularization}).
\subsection{Transformer}\label{Transformer}
Core mechanisms of Transformer used are introduced below including: Global attention, Scaled dot-product
attention, Position-wise feed-forward network.

2 Related work
2.1 Prescription-Pharmacy Transcription
Electronic Transcription of Prescriptions (ETP) in health care has been sug-
gested to have positive impact for prescribing process in safety, quality, effi-
ciency. [3]. The detailed ultimate goal of implementing transcription in dis-
pensing was to achieve the following: reduce medical errors, decrease pharmacy
costs, improve both prescriber and pharmacy efficiency, reduce phone calls be-
tween pharmacists and physicians, reduce data entry, and expedite prescription
refill requests.[31]. 23% of medication errors were transcribing errors including
missing information and discrepancy between the prescribed medications and
pharmacy directions [21].
Prior work mainly focused on prescription extraction. Earlier prescription
extraction models mainly relied on handwritten rules or linguistic signals in
prescription free text. MeLEE was built to extract, structure, and encode clin-
ical information from textual patient reports [8]. MetaMap extracted medi-
cation information with advantage of external sources: UMLS, cTAKES, and
Medline[1]. FABLE employed a semi-supervised machine learning approach
based on Conditional Random Fields (CRFs) to capitalize on unlabeled data to
enhance recognition of sparsely represented, lexically and syntactically diverse
medication entities[29].
Task of text normalization/ simplification also served vital role in tran-
scription to avoid ambiguity for experts (pharmacists) and to understand the
1
meanings easier for layman (patients), which has attracted many researches.
Rule-based replacement methods had advantage of extra domain knowledge like
UMLS [25][12]. Machine translation(MT) got popular recent years for without
the need of explicitly defined rules using convolutional or recurrent neural net-
work on large corpus [36][26][30].

the base learners since the error can be reduced in the aggregated predictor
through majority voting [5]. For the same model structure of transformer mod-
ule, parameterized module will still show diversity with the existing of dropout
probability and different initialization settings. Odd number of modules can be
trained independently and final output sentences will be the majority voting of
results of these modules, which can also indicates the confidence of modules for
outcome.
3.3 Numerical Checker and Back-off
Dosage, Frequency and Duration are three fields that usually contain unmod-
ified numerical information during the transcription process. However, neural
machine translation model based on conditional probability sometimes can’t
have full guarantee on this, which is much more crucial and sensitive compared
with machine translation tasks. When the generated output comes to Numerical
Checker, decision is made based on whether the candidate sentences made up
new numbers never appeared in source sentence. Inverse checking process isn’t
applied with the season that pharmacists will add some additional information
upon prescription sometimes. Graceful back-off path is taken once wrong trans-
lation are found, skipping neural machine translation to avoid possible mistakes,
though it might not follow pharmacy style.
3.4 Rule-based Regularization
Some pharmacy-style normalization and patient-friendly simplification are ap-
plied to transcribe e-prescription automatically and more efficiently in prac-
tice for pharmacists. Handwritten rules are designed based on linguistic sig-
nals found in sample dataset by expertise from two pharmacists, including verb
complement, style of form, route, frequency and duration fields. Some examples
shown in Tables 3.4.
